Music and Dance

Music and dance are culture expressions in Lesotho, includingtraditional forms like the enegetic men`s Mohobelo dance and rhythimc women`s Mokhibo dance. Modern music thrives with Famo, a popular genre featuring accordions and oil-can drums, and growing Basotho hip-hop and AfroPop scenes.


Mohobelo

An energetic dance for men that features string, leaping, and high stomps, creating a vibrant and powerful performance.


Mokhibo

A women`s dance performed while kneeling characterized by grasceful, rhythimic movements of the hands and body`s rising and falling.


Mokorotlo

A men`s dance performed on important occasions, such as meetings and inspections.


Maqekha

A special dance that is part of the girl`s initiation rites.


Ditolobonya

A song and dance performed exclusively by women and girls


Ntlamu

A male dance similar to the Zulu ndlamu, where dancers emphasize stamping and handclapping. Dancers often accessorize their outfits with items that enhance the choreopraphy.


Lebollo

Initiation rites for boys and girls that invovle singing, dancing, praising, and ululation.


Wedding songs

Songs perfomed by maids of the bride and groom during weddings.
